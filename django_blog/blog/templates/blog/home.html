Nigeria’s Underappreciation of the Tech Industry

Nigeria has produced globally recognized tech startups like Flutterwave and Paystack, yet the ecosystem continues to suffer from a lack of support. With a youthful population and immense talent, the potential is there—but the recognition is not.

Why Tech Matters

The Brookings Institution reports that Africa’s digital economy could contribute $180 billion to the continent’s GDP by 2025. Nigeria stands to gain massively if the tech sector is empowered with policy and infrastructure.

  • Tech provides scalable employment for Nigeria's youth
  • It fosters innovation in sectors like health and agriculture
  • It positions Nigeria on the global digital map

The Consequences of Neglect

By sidelining tech, Nigeria risks growing unemployment, loss of global competitiveness, and brain drain as skilled developers move abroad for better opportunities.

Solutions

  1. Tech-friendly policies: Clear regulations, digital rights protection
  2. Education: Fund CS programs in schools and support local bootcamps
  3. Partnerships: Enable collaborations between tech startups and government
  4. Infrastructure: Build reliable internet, data centers, and developer spaces

Conclusion

Nigeria’s future lies in innovation. Supporting tech now means securing progress, employment, and relevance for years to come.
